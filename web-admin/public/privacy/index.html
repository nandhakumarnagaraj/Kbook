Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how KhanaBook collects, uses, stores, and protects personal and business data when you use the KhanaBook Android app and the KhanaBook cloud backend for restaurant billing, POS, reporting, and sync services.

Effective: 1 May 2026 Version: 1.0 Applies to the Android App and Cloud Backend
Offline-first by design. KhanaBook stores working data on the device and syncs with the cloud when connectivity is available.

Operator

KhanaBook is operated by Piquant Consultancy Services, Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India.

Data Ownership

Restaurants retain ownership of their business data. KhanaBook processes that data to provide billing, sync, reporting, security, and support features.

No Ads

KhanaBook does not display third-party ads and does not use third-party advertising trackers.

1. Introduction

KhanaBook is restaurant billing and POS software that includes an Android application and a cloud backend hosted at https://kbook.iadv.cloud.

By using KhanaBook, you consent to the collection and use of data as described in this Privacy Policy, subject to your rights under applicable law.

2. Data Controller and Processing Roles

For the purposes of applicable data protection laws, Piquant Consultancy Services acts as the data controller for account, authentication, support, security, and platform operations data collected through KhanaBook.

For restaurant operational data entered into KhanaBook, such as menu, billing, invoice, payment, stock, and staff records, KhanaBook primarily acts as a software provider and processor on behalf of the restaurant using the service.

3. Data We Collect

Account and business data

  • Name, mobile number, email address, login identifiers, and role information
  • Restaurant profile data such as business name, address, GST details, printer settings, and tax configuration

Billing and operational data

  • Orders, invoices, bill items, bill payments, stock logs, menu items, categories, and related timestamps
  • Optional customer contact details used for invoice sharing or order-related communication

Technical and security data

  • Device identifiers used for sync and session protection, app version, request metadata, IP address, and security logs
  • Authentication events, OTP challenge metadata, and token revocation or session integrity records

4. App Permissions

  • Camera: Used for OCR-based menu scanning and import workflows
  • Bluetooth: Used to discover and connect supported thermal printers
  • Location on older Android versions: Required by Android for Bluetooth discovery on Android 11 and below
  • Internet: Required for login, sync, support flows, and cloud services

5. How We Use Data

  • To provide restaurant billing, POS, reporting, printing, and sync functionality
  • To authenticate users, secure accounts, validate sessions, and prevent abuse or fraud
  • To generate invoices, tax records, operational reports, and payment records
  • To support features such as Google sign-in, OCR import, payment return flows, and invoice sharing
  • To monitor reliability, investigate incidents, and improve service quality

6. Third-Party Services

We share data only where needed to deliver specific platform features.

  • Google Sign-In: Used when you choose Google-based authentication
  • Google ML Kit: Used for OCR menu scanning workflows
  • WhatsApp / Meta: Used when you choose to send invoices or OTP-related messages through WhatsApp channels
  • Easebuzz: Used for payment processing, status updates, and payment return or webhook workflows
  • Infrastructure providers: Used to host and operate the KhanaBook backend and supporting systems

We do not sell or rent personal data.

7. Storage, Sync, and Security

  • KhanaBook is designed to work offline first. Local app data is stored on-device and later synchronized with the backend.
  • The Android app uses encrypted local database storage and keystore-backed secret storage for sensitive local data.
  • API communication is intended to occur over HTTPS/TLS.
  • The backend uses JWT-based authentication, device and session integrity checks, tenant-scoped data handling, and rate limiting on sensitive flows.

9. International Data Transfers

Some third-party services used by KhanaBook, including services operated by Google or Meta, may process data outside India. Where this happens, we take reasonable steps to ensure such transfers are handled in compliance with applicable data protection law.

10. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion, subject to legal and operational retention obligations
  • Request export of relevant data where technically feasible

11. What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell personal data
  • We do not display third-party ads inside the app
  • We do not collect card numbers, CVV values, or UPI PINs through KhanaBook
  • We do not use third-party advertising trackers

12. Children's Privacy

KhanaBook is intended for restaurant owners, operators, and staff. It is not directed to children under 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.
